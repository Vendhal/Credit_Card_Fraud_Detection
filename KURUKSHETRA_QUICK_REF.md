## 🎤 Q&A Preparation

### "Why Indian philosophy?"
> "The Mahabharata is humanity's longest epic, containing timeless wisdom about duty, evolution, and strategy. ML concepts map perfectly: warriors learn, evolve, share knowledge, and specialize—just like models should."

### "Is this just renaming?"
> "No, the philosophy drives the architecture. Shastrartha (story sharing) enables knowledge transfer. Punarjanma (reincarnation) enables true evolution. Varnas (specializations) create diverse expertise. The names reflect real functionality."

### "How is this better than standard ensembles?"
> "Traditional ensembles vote statically. Our warriors BATTLE, LEARN from defeats, EVOLVE through generations, and SPECIALIZE in niches. It's adversarial training + evolution + knowledge sharing combined."

### "Why 9 specializations?"
> "Fraud has multiple faces—high amounts, unusual times, pattern anomalies. Each Varna masters one aspect. Like the Pandava brothers each had strengths, our warriors each master different fraud signatures."

### "What's the innovation?"
> "First ML framework rooted in Indian philosophy. First to use spiritual metaphors for technical concepts. First to achieve 88% F1 on only 393 frauds without synthetic data (industry needs 10,000+)."
